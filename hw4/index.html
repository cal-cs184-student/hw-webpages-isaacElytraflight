Part 1: Masses and springs

As we can see in the following images, the cloth is initially set up with masses and springs. It was a bit of a struggle to get the renderer to run at first due to antiquated dependencies, but eventually I got it working.

The shear springs are on the diagonals of the grid, and the structural springs are on the edges between lattice points. The bending springs are on the edges between lattice points of distance 2 away.

This makes physical sense, as the shear springs are responsible for the cloth "bunching up" when a shear force is applied, and the structural springs are responsible for the cloth "stretching" when a tension force is applied.

The bending springs are likely 2 apart so that the cloth can bend up and down, rather than just side to side.
